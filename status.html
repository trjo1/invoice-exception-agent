2. What this agent does — in one paragraph

The agent watches the buyer's procure-to-pay process across SAP, Ariba, and ServiceNow. When an exception event arises — invoice doesn't match the PO, quantity variance, missing approval, duplicate invoice, fraud signal, vendor master gap, cross-currency mismatch, tax field problem — the agent classifies it into one of 12 named categories, retrieves the relevant policy + history + master data, produces a recommended action with rationale and counterfactual, drafts the supplier or internal communication needed to resolve it, routes to the right human at the right HITL tier, and executes the cross-system write-back actions only after human approval. Every step is instrumented for Stage 9 measurement: auto-pass rate, HITL accuracy, cost per task, cross-system error rate, audit-finding count.

Why build this rather than use an accelerator
Cross-system orchestration with bespoke business rules defaults to BUILD per our framework. No single accelerator (SynOps, Gemini Enterprise, Agentforce) covers SAP + Ariba + ServiceNow cleanly without locking the buyer to MS or Google. The orchestration logic IS the moat.

3. The workflow — node by node

4. The 13 exception scenarios the classifier handles

Every invoice the agent sees gets one of these 13 labels. The classifier is anchored on the invoice itself, with cross-case context (vendor master, PO, GR, history) as supplementary evidence — not the other way around. Each label has a typical recommended action and routing tier; the per-case tier can still escalate based on confidence and dollar exposure.

1 auto-pass class 10 buyer / team review 2 supervisor / fraud / treasury All routing is rules-based, not LLM-decided
Clean — no exception
none
Invoice matches its PO + GR cleanly. No smart cross-case signal fired. Vendor in good standing, currency/payment-terms aligned, totals within tolerance.
Tier 1 · auto-pass auto_resolve
Price variance vs. PO
three_way_match_price_variance
Invoiced unit price differs from the PO-authorized price beyond the tolerance band. Either a supplier overcharge or a missed PO amendment.
Tier 2 · buyer request_supplier_credit_memo
Quantity variance vs. GR
three_way_match_quantity_variance
Invoice claims a quantity that doesn't match the goods receipt (short or over-delivery). Distinct from price — same unit price, wrong count.
Tier 2 · buyer escalate_for_short_delivery
PO reference missing
missing_po
Invoice has no PO number, or references a PO that doesn't exist in the master. Common with emergency / retroactive PO scenarios.
Tier 2 · buyer escalate_for_retroactive_po
Goods receipt not yet posted
missing_goods_receipt
Invoice arrived but the warehouse hasn't confirmed receipt. Hold and re-check on the next polling cycle — don't pay against an unreceived shipment.
Tier 2 · buyer hold_for_goods_receipt
Authorization gap
missing_approval
Spend exceeds the PO's authorized total, or capex routed to an opex approver. Needs a higher signing tier before posting.
Tier 3 · VP finance route_to_vp_finance_approval
Duplicate invoice
duplicate_invoice
Same supplier invoice number already booked, or the PO is already paid in full. Smart signal looks for "DUPLICATE:" / "PO ALREADY FULLY PAID."
Tier 2 · buyer request_supplier_correction
Fraud signal
fraud_signal
Vendor bank account just changed and invoice is high-value, split-invoicing pattern detected, or sanctions hit (OFAC SDN). Halts pay run, escalates to fraud team.
Tier 3 · fraud team escalate_to_fraud
Vendor not in master
vendor_master_gap
Supplier's tax ID / name not found in the vendor master file. Could be onboarding lag or a fictitious supplier — needs vendor master team review.
Tier 2 · vendor master team route_to_vendor_master_onboarding
Currency mismatch
cross_currency_mismatch
PO authorized in one currency, invoice billed in another. Could be legitimate per FX clause, or an FX-rate dispute requiring treasury sign-off.
Tier 2 · buyer / treasury escalate_for_fx_review
Tax field anomaly
tax_field_mismatch
Tax rate, jurisdiction, or amount on the invoice doesn't match what the buyer's tax rules expect (e.g. EU VAT on a US-only supplier; missing IGST on Indian invoice).
Tier 2 · buyer request_supplier_correction
Payment terms mismatch
payment_term_mismatch
Invoice asserts NET-15 when the PO/MSA specifies NET-30 (or vice versa). Catches suppliers trying to accelerate payment terms without contract change.
Tier 2 · buyer request_supplier_correction
Other / non-standard
other
Catch-all for exceptions that don't fit the 12 named categories. Defaults to buyer review with the agent's rationale + suggested next action.
Tier 2 · buyer other

Each scenario maps to 1–2 of the 16 RecommendedAction values. The recommendation is the "what to do," the routing tier is the "who decides." Decoupling them is what lets the same exception escalate to fraud / treasury / VP when the dollar exposure or confidence warrants it, without rewriting the classifier.

6. Human-in-the-loop touchpoints

HITL is a first-class part of the agent, not an afterthought. Three tiers — designed per the Coordination & Orchestration reference architecture. The HITL queue has its own SLA and named owner.

Tier 1 — Auto-action

No human approval needed

Triggered when: classification = none AND confidence above per-class threshold AND action is read-only or non-money-moving.
  • Clean 3-way matches that pass all checks (GTC-001 anchor case)
  • Internal status updates / notifications
  • PO ETA updates (informational)
  • Target: 70–85% of invoices land here once the agent is tuned to a buyer's volume
Tier 2 — Human approval gate

Approver reviews and approves / modifies / rejects

Routed to the right named role: AP clerk, buyer, vendor master team, VP Finance — based on case category + dollar exposure.
  • Price + quantity variances above threshold (GTC-002, GTC-006)
  • Missing approvals (GTC-011) — routes to the named approver
  • Non-master vendor (GTC-004) — routes to vendor master team with onboarding draft
  • EU VAT corrections (GTC-008) — routes to AP clerk with supplier correction draft
  • Cross-currency reviews (GTC-010) — routes to AP clerk with FX validation
  • Supplier delivery delays (GTC-009) — routes to buyer with acknowledgement + internal note drafts
  • Retroactive maverick-spend invoices (GTC-005)
Tier 3 — Escalation

Supervisor + fraud team review

Routed when: fraud signal triggered, threshold violations, cross-system inconsistencies, novel patterns.
  • Duplicate invoices on already-paid POs (GTC-003)
  • Split-invoice fraud patterns (GTC-007)
  • Cross-process audit findings (raised weekly by a governance team)
  • Any case the agent halts on with policy-defined cause
Non-negotiable
External counterparty communications NEVER auto-send. Money-moving actions always require Tier 2+ approval, regardless of confidence. The HITL gate is the safety surface — losing it means losing the agent's defensibility.
